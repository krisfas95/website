About Me

Kristina Fasulo



My name is Kristina Fasulo and I am an aspriring student with dreams of working in the marketing communications, advertisting, graphic design, and/or social media fields. I was born January 13, 1995 in Staten Island, New York. I am currently 20 years old and attending The College of New Jersey for Marketing with a minor in Interactive Multimedia.

I grew up in the small town of Stewartsville, New Jersey and would always dream to be a Rockette. I have been dancing for 17 years since I was three years old. Besides dancing, my parents noticed at an early age that I was very talented with computers. It helped that my father is in IT, but they noticed I would be able to solve most problems and excelled in the courses during my early years. I decided to keep up on my technological skills with taking more advance courses throughout my high school career. Throughout those four years, I have acquired many skills such as the basics of Adobe Photoshop, Fireworks, InDesign, Premiere, and other similar software. I also continues to expand my horizons by taking many art courses including painting, while getting involved with the television station and founding and starting a club, "Video Yearbook Club"

After falling in love with The College of New Jersey's campus, I decided to accept and go in as an undecided, open-options Business student. With nothing in mind besides dance, I had no idea what I wanted to do. After my first semester at The College of New Jersey, I realized how much I missed my graphic design courses and decided to take interactive multimedia class and fell in love. I picked up a minor before I declared a major. Later after much analysis, I decided to declare in marketing as well as give up my dreams of dancing for a career path.

After two years of rigurous dance team experience, when I realized I no longer wanted to choose that life for myself, I quit the dance team in aspiration of other involvement. I quickly got involved with the tv station on campus, Lions Television and the peer mentor group for the School of Business. Later, I found a job at the bookstore, accepted to be an Ambassador, which only 35 out of about 400 applicants get accepted, and rushed for the new sorority and became a founding sister of Alpha Xi Delta.

Besides all the leadership and activities on campus, I love to craft, hangout with friends, and help others with various projects when I can. This includes planning my sorority's Fall Date Party, editing packages for LionsTelevision, watching New York Ranger games with my dad, and binge watching One Tree Hill on Netflix.

After much analysis and personality-trait quizzes, I decided on a major and a minor, which blend quite eloquiently. This makes me beyond passionate and excited for what I want to do with my life. My anticipated graduation date is Spring of 2017. I hope to further my skillset, achieve more internship experience, to further my workforce experience.
